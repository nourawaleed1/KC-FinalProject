Petroleum

Petroleum, also called crude oil, is a fossil fuel. Like coal and natural gas, petroleum was formed from the remains of ancient marine organisms, such as plants, algae, and bacteria.

Why is it important

It has been used as fuel for our transport, a fuel to generate electricity to light our homes, run factories and machines, a raw material to produce fertiliser to increase food production and produce plastic

Black Gold

Petroleum is called black gold because of its great commercial importance. Many useful substances are obtained from petroleum and natural gas. These are termed as 'Petrochemicals'.
